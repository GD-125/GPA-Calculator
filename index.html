Understanding GPA, CGPA & SGPA

Welcome to our comprehensive GPA Calculator tool! Whether you're calculating your current academic standing or planning future course loads, understanding the differences between GPA, CGPA, and SGPA is essential for tracking your academic progress.

What is GPA?

Grade Point Average (GPA) is a standardized measurement of academic achievement.

It represents the average value of the accumulated final grades earned in courses over time, ranging typically from 0 to 4.0 or 0 to 10.0, depending on the educational system.

Purpose:

⚡serves as a universal metric for academic performance, allowing institutions to assess student achievement consistently.

⚡It's often used for scholarship eligibility, honors determination, and entrance requirements for higher education.

What is CGPA?

Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) represents the overall academic performance across all semesters or terms completed.

It is calculated by dividing the sum of grade points earned in all courses by the total credit hours attempted.

Purpose:

⚡Provides a comprehensive view of your academic journey, reflecting your overall performance throughout your educational program.

⚡It's particularly important for graduation requirements, job applications, and higher education admissions.

What is SGPA?

Semester Grade Point Average (SGPA) is the average of grade points obtained in all courses registered in a specific semester.

Each course is weighted according to its credit hours.

Purpose:

⚡SGPA helps track academic performance in a particular semester, allowing students to monitor term-specific progress.

⚡It's useful for identifying strengths and weaknesses in specific academic periods and making adjustments to study strategies.
